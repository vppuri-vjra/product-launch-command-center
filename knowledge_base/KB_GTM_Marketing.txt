# Project Nightingale — Go-To-Market & Marketing Playbook
## Last Updated: March 5, 2026

---

## Campaign Overview

### Campaign Name: "Checkout in a Snap, Earn While You Shop"
### Campaign Duration: April 1 - April 30, 2026
### Total Marketing Budget: $65,000

---

## Channel Strategy

### Email Marketing ($5,000)
- March 25: Teaser email — "Something big is coming" — targeting all active users (estimated 180,000 recipients)
  - Subject line A/B test: "Your shopping experience is about to change" vs "Big news dropping April 15"
  - Expected open rate: 28-32%
- April 8: Early access invite to power users (top 10% by purchase frequency, ~18,000 users)
  - These users get access on April 12 (3 days before public launch)
- April 15: Launch day email — "It's here. Meet the new checkout + Perks."
  - Includes animated GIF showing one-tap checkout
  - CTA: "Update your app now"
- April 22: Referral campaign email — "Share Perks, Get Perks"
  - Referral mechanism: unique referral code per user, 500 bonus points for both referrer and referee
  - Expected referral conversion: 8-12% of recipients share their code

### Paid Social ($45,000)
- Platforms: Instagram ($18,000), TikTok ($15,000), YouTube ($12,000)
- Creative formats:
  - Instagram: Carousel ads showing old checkout vs new checkout (before/after), Reels showing one-tap checkout in action
  - TikTok: 15-second "POV: you checkout in 3 seconds" trend-format video, influencer partnership with 3 micro-influencers (50K-150K followers)
  - YouTube: 30-second pre-roll ad showing checkout + Perks tier animation
- Targeting: Existing app users (retargeting), lookalike audiences based on high-LTV customers, interest-based targeting (online shopping, deals, rewards programs)
- Expected impressions: 4.5-6 million
- Expected CPA (cost per app update): $2.50-3.50

### PR & Media ($8,000)
- Press kit distributed to 25 tech and e-commerce publications on April 1
- Embargo lifts April 10 — coordinated reviews go live
- Target publications: TechCrunch, The Verge, Retail Dive, Mobile Commerce Daily
- Exclusive feature offered to TechCrunch (first look at Perks loyalty program)
- Press kit includes: product screenshots, executive quotes, beta test data (conversion improvement stats), one-pager on Perks program

### App Store Optimization ($2,000)
- Updated app store listing with new screenshots showing checkout redesign and Perks dashboard
- Updated app description highlighting key features
- Release notes emphasizing speed improvement and loyalty program
- Keyword optimization for: "fast checkout," "loyalty rewards," "one tap payment"

### Organic Social ($5,000 — primarily for content creation)
- Twitter/X: Daily countdown posts starting April 8 ("7 days until your checkout gets a major upgrade")
- Instagram Stories: Behind-the-scenes of the design process, team celebration moments
- LinkedIn: Thought leadership post from VP Product on building loyalty programs (targeting B2B audience, potential press pickup)
- Community: Reddit AMA with Product Manager (r/ecommerce, r/startups) scheduled April 16

## Key Messaging

### Primary Message
"Checkout in seconds. Earn rewards with every purchase. App v3.0 is here."

### Supporting Messages
1. Speed: "New one-tap checkout cuts your purchase time in half."
2. Rewards: "Introducing Perks — earn points on every order, unlock free shipping and exclusive products."
3. Simplicity: "No account needed. Guest checkout lets you buy in 3 taps."
4. Trust: "Tested by 4,200 beta users. Faster, easier, and more reliable."

### Key Stats for External Use
- Checkout time reduced from 72 seconds to 38 seconds (47% faster)
- Cart abandonment dropped from 34% to 19% in beta
- 4,200 beta testers over 3 weeks
- NPS score for new checkout: 72 (up from 54)

### Messaging DON'Ts
- Do NOT share specific revenue projections externally
- Do NOT mention competitor names in any marketing materials
- Do NOT make claims about "fastest checkout" without qualifier ("up to X% faster")
- Do NOT share internal budget figures

## Sales Enablement

### Key Talking Points for Sales Team
1. "Our new checkout converts 18-22% better than the old flow. That directly impacts your clients' bottom line."
2. "The Perks loyalty program auto-enrolls users at Bronze tier — zero friction. Clients don't need to 'sell' the program to their users."
3. "We're the only platform with one-tap checkout + guest checkout + a loyalty program. Competitors make you choose."
4. "This isn't vaporware — 4,200 real users tested it and the data proves it works."

### Objection Handling
- "My client's customers won't sign up for another loyalty program" → Auto-enrollment. No sign-up required. They earn points from their first purchase.
- "How is this different from Competitor A?" → Tiered rewards drive 3x more repeat purchases than flat points systems (based on industry data from Bond Loyalty Report 2025).
- "What if there are bugs at launch?" → We've done 3 weeks of beta testing with 4,200 users. Staged rollout (5% → 25% → 50% → 100%) means we catch issues before they affect everyone. Automated rollback in under 15 minutes if anything goes wrong.
